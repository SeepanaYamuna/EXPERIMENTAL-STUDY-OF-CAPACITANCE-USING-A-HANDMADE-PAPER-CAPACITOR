##  Description

This project focuses on the design and fabrication of a DIY capacitor using aluminium foil as conductive plates and paper as the dielectric medium. The objective is to experimentally study capacitance, energy storage, and the influence of physical parameters such as plate area, dielectric thickness, and material properties. The work bridges theoretical electrostatics with practical implementation, providing insight into real-world non-idealities of passive components.

---

##  Working Principle

A capacitor stores electrical energy in the form of an electric field established between two conductive plates separated by an insulating dielectric. When a voltage is applied, equal and opposite charges accumulate on the plates, creating an electric field across the dielectric.

The ability of the capacitor to store charge is defined by its capacitance:

```math
C = \frac{\varepsilon A}{d}
```

Where:

* ( C ): Capacitance
* Permittivity of the dielectric material
* ( A ): Effective overlapping area of the plates
* ( d ): Separation between the plates

In this project, paper serves as the dielectric, and its thickness and material properties directly influence capacitance.

---

##  Construction Details

The capacitor is constructed by layering aluminium foil sheets separated by paper. Multiple layers are stacked or rolled to increase the effective plate area while maintaining a compact structure.

Key aspects:

* Uniform layering ensures consistent electric field distribution
* Tight rolling reduces air gaps (which affect dielectric behavior)
* Proper insulation prevents short-circuit between plates

---

##  Observations & Analysis

* Capacitance increases with larger plate area due to increased charge storage capability
* Increasing dielectric thickness reduces capacitance as electric field strength decreases
* Multi-layer configuration significantly enhances capacitance in a compact form

###  Non-Ideal Effects

* Leakage current due to imperfect dielectric (paper absorbs moisture)
* Parasitic resistance and capacitance
* Variations due to manual construction

---

##  Key Learnings

* Practical limitations of real dielectric materials
* Difference between ideal capacitor models and real implementations
* Importance of material selection in analog circuit design
